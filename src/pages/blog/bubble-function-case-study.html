Bubble Function: A Game Design Case Study

November 10, 2019 2 min read

In 2019, I led the design and development of "Bubble Function," an educational math game for 9-12-year-olds. This project was born from a need to replace Mangahigh's discontinued "Algebra Meltdown," a game that, while popular, was deemed too challenging for its target audience.

Mangahigh, an online learning platform, sought a new game to engage young learners and help them grasp the concept of function machines. After careful consideration of user feedback, we decided to focus on the function machine element and simplify the overall gameplay.

The Design Process

My design process began with extensive research and ideation. I explored various concepts, eventually settling on a unique blend of function machines and the classic bubble shooter mechanic. This innovative approach aimed to make learning both fun and intuitive.

Paper prototyping played a crucial role in early development. I sketched out puzzle levels and tested them with colleagues to gauge their playability and gather initial feedback. This iterative process ensured that the core gameplay mechanics were engaging and accessible.

Building upon the core gameplay, I created a comprehensive game design document (GDD). This document served as a blueprint for the development team, outlining all game features, mechanics, and design specifications.

Development and Testing

The development phase involved close collaboration with a cross-functional team, including in-house and remote developers, a remote artist/animator, and a dedicated games designer. We followed an agile development methodology, ensuring flexibility and rapid iteration.

Throughout the development cycle, rigorous testing was conducted. I personally led in-school testing sessions with a diverse group of students, observing their gameplay and gathering valuable feedback. This invaluable input allowed us to fine-tune the game's difficulty, improve the user interface, and address any potential challenges.

Release and Beyond

After a successful development phase, "Bubble Function" was launched on the Mangahigh platform. The game received positive feedback and has since been enjoyed by countless students.

Even after the initial release, I continued to support the project, ensuring a smooth handover to the team responsible for ongoing development and maintenance.

"Bubble Function" is a testament to the power of collaborative design, user-centered development, and a commitment to creating engaging and effective learning experiences.
